Fort Pulaski National Monument

Southeast > Savannah > Fort Pulaski NM

Highlights:

Civil War landmark with ranger tours, musket & canon firings plus riverfront hiking & biking trails

General Information

Hours:
Fort Pulaski National Monument is open year round from 9am to 5pm, except for New Years, Thanksgiving, and Christmas Days.
The Visitor Center is open from 9am to 5pm and historic Fort Pulaski is open 9am to 4:45pm
Fees:
Per Person: $10 valid for 7 consecutive days. Youth 15 and under are admitted free of charge
Accepts America The Beautiful Pass
Pet Policy:
Pets allowed
Seasons:
All year. Weather is hot and humid during the summer
Rating (1-5):
★★★★

How to Get There

Head east on E Bay St toward E Upper Factors Walk. Continue onto General McIntosh Blvd. Use the left 2 lanes to turn left onto E President St. Continue straight to stay on E President St. Continue onto Islands Expy. Keep left at the fork, follow signs for US-80 E and merge onto US-80 E/Islands Expy.

Overview

Named for Revolutionary War hero Casimir Pulaski, it was here that General Robert E. Lee was first assigned after graduating from West Point. Today, you can explore the fort's colossal ramparts, intimidating stone towers, drawbridges and moats. Frequent re-enactments allow you to see what life in the fort was like back in its heyday. After you've gotten your fill of Pulaski, head to the nearby Tybee Lighthouse (situated about 5 miles east of the fort) for some fantastic views of the coast and a stroll along the barrier island's 5-mile-long beach. Recent visitors said the fort is a must-visit for history buffs and strongly recommended taking one of the ranger-led tours. Fort tours, which usually last 45 minutes to an hour, are offered daily 11am and 3pm.
