Clingmans Dome Trail

South > Tennessee > Great Smoky Mountain NP > Clingmans Dome Trail

Highlights:

Highest point in the Great Smoky Mountains National Park and state of Tennessee providing excellent mountain views and high elevation spruce-fir forest

General Information

Length:
1.2 miles roundtrip
Elevation gain:
331 feet
Pet Policy:
Pets not allowed
Seasons:
Spring, Summer, Fall. Road leading to Clingmans Dome is closed seasonal typically from early December through late March, and whenever weather conditions require
Trailhead:
Parking lot at the end of Clingmans Dome Road
Rating (1-5):
★★★★

How to Get There

At 6,643 feet, Clingmans Dome is the highest point in the Great Smoky Mountains National Park and state of Tennessee. The observation tower on the summit of Clingmans Dome offers spectacular 360Â° views of the Smokies and beyond for visitors willing to climb the steep half-mile walk to the tower at the top. Overview

Clouds, precipitation, and cold temperatures are common at Clingmans Dome. Temperatures at the dome can be 10 -20 degrees Fahrenheit cooler than in the surrounding lowlands. In fact, the cool, wet conditions on Clingmans Dome's summit make the spruce-fir forest that grows there a coniferous rainforest. Proper preparation is essential for a good visit. Dress in layers and be sure to bring a jacket, even in summer. Although Clingmans Dome Observation Tower is open year-round, the road leading to it is closed seasonal typically from early December through late March, and whenever weather conditions require

Overview

It's seven miles to the end of Clingmans Dome Road and there are scenic pullouts with endless views of ridges and valleys along the way. The road ends in a large parking area from which a 0.5 mile trail leads to the summit. The trail is paved but steep, and leads to an observation tower on top. Pets and bicycles are not permitted on the paved trail to the observation tower, or on any other trails in the area. A bike rack is located near the beginning of the paved trail to park bikes while walking to the top. You will need to bring a lock with you to secure your bike.

Overview

Although the trail is paved, it is too steep to be wheelchair accessible. Besides the trail to the summit, there are several trails that start on Clingmans Dome Road and parking area. The Appalachian Trail (AT) crosses Clingmans Dome, marking the highest point along its journey from Georgia to Maine. The Forney Ridge Trail leads to Andrews Bald, a high-elevation grassy bald.
