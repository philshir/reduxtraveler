Mount Rainier National Park

Rating ★★★★★

National park preserving all of Mount Rainier, a 14,411-foot (4,392 m) stratovolcano, surrounded by valleys, waterfalls, subalpine meadows, and old-growth forest

Mount St. Helens National Volcanic Monument

Rating ★★★★★

Best known as the site of the historic 1980 volcanic eruption, the Monument offers a variety of recreation opportunities, including the chance to climb to the summit of the most active volcano in the Cascade Range

North Cascades National Park

Rating ★★★★★

Park features the rugged mountain peaks of the North Cascades Range, the most expansive glacial system in the contiguous United States, the headwaters of numerous waterways, and vast forests with the highest degree of flora biodiversity of any American national park.

Olympic National Park

Rating ★★★★★

National park highlightng four regions: the Pacific coastline, alpine areas, the west-side temperate rainforest, and the forests of the drier east side and three distinct ecosystems, including subalpine forest and wildflower meadow, temperate forest, and the rugged Pacific coast
