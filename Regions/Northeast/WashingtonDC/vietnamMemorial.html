Vietnam Veterans Memorial

Northeast > Washington, DC > Vietnam Veterans Memorial

Highlights:

Monument to Vietnam War service, featuring a wall with over 58,000 names of dead & missing soldiers

General Information

Hours:
24/7
Fees:

Pet Policy:
Pets allowed
Seasons:
All year
Rating (1-5):
★★★★

How to Get There

Head southeast on Scott Cir NW toward Rhode Island Ave NW. Exit the traffic circle onto Rhode Island Ave NW. Use the left lane to turn slightly right onto M St NW. Turn left at the 2nd cross street onto 19th St NW. Turn right onto Constitution Ave. NW. Use the left 2 lanes to turn left onto Henry Bacon Dr NW.

The City

One of the most moving war memorials, the Vietnam Veterans Memorial – or "the Wall," as it's commonly referred to – is a long black granite wall with the names of more than 58,000 Americans who perished during the Vietnam War emblazoned on its surface. If you're looking for a specific person, keep in mind that the soldiers' names are ordered by the date they died, not alphabetically. Also, reviewers recommend using the attraction's name books and visiting during the day when there's ample sunlight.
