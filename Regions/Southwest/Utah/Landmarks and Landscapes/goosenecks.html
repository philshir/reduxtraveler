Goosenecks State Park

Southwest > Utah > Goosenecks SP

Highlights:

Spectacular entrenched meanders of the San Juan River, one of the best examples of this feature anywhere in the world. Viewable from a roadside overlook, at which camping is also permitted

How to Get There

From Page, AZ (151mi) Follow AZ-98 E to US-160 E in Navajo County. Take US-163 N to UT-261 N in San Juan County. Drive to UT-316 W

The San Juan River is slow-moving and flows through a relatively shallow red rock canyon with many wide curves; more of these convolutions can be seen in the nearby Goosenecks State Park, reached by a four mile paved side road (UT 316) that branches off UT 261 a little way north of town.

The park, to which entry was for many years free but is now charged at $5 per vehicle (in 2020), has just one extended viewpoint of several huge river bends, now flowing one thousand feet below ground level in a deep canyon formed by a series of stepped cliffs and terraces, a feature recognized as one of the best examples of entrenched river meanders in the world. At the park, the waters flow through 5 miles of canyon whilst progressing westwards only one mile. Beyond the visible meanders, the river continues to twist and turn as the canyon deepens, before joining Lake Powell after about 35 miles.
