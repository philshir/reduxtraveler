Casa Grande Ruins National Monument

Southwest > Arizona > Casa Grande Ruins NM

Highlights:

Get on I-10 E from W Jefferson St. Follow I-10 E to AZ-387 N in Pinal County. Take exit 185 from I-10 E. Follow AZ-387 N and AZ-87 S to W Ruins Dr in Coolidge.

The Bend

Casa Grande Ruins National Monument contains an imposing 4-storey building dating from the late Hohokam period, probably 14th century and contemporary with other well preserved ruins in Arizona such as the Tonto and Montezuma Castle monuments. It is located just north of Coolidge and about 15 miles from the larger town of Casa Grande. A door at the back of the visitor center leads to the Casa Grande itself, at the middle of a rectangular area that used to be a walled compound containing several other smaller buildings.

The 'Great House' can be seen from some distance away owing to the flatness of the terrain, and has a rather curious appearance from afar as the structure is protected from the harsh desert sun by a large metal roof supported by four great pillars The scale of the ruin is best appreciated from close up - it is 60 feet by 40 feet wide at the base and has caliche walls over a meter thick. Although visitors are not allowed into the building owing to its delicate state, much can be seen from outside including details of the construction with wooden beams supporting the clay walls, and various internal features such as stairways and windows.
