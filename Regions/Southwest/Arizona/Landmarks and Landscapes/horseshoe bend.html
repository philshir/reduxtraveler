Horseshoe Bend

Southwest > Arizona > Horseshoe Bend

Highlights:

One of the most spectacular and oft-photographed places in Arizona, where the Colorado river curves round by 270 degrees where the water in the Colorado is slow moving, greenish blue in color and edged by thin strips of bright green vegetation - a striking contrast to the red-brown Navajo sandstone cliffs at either side..

How to Get There

From Page, AZ, head northeast on S Navajo Dr toward S Lake Powell Blvd. Turn right at the 1st cross street onto S Lake Powell Blvd. Turn left onto US-89 S. At the traffic circle, take the 2nd exit onto US-89. Turn right.

The Bend

Where the Colorado River flows through the 1,000 foot deep ravine for 15 miles, until the cliffs open out at Lees Ferry. Apart from viewpoints around the US 89 bridge and the dam itself, the canyon can be seen via two trails - the lightly used and rather strenuous Spencer Trail starting at Lees Ferry or the much easier and very popular half mile path to Horseshoe Bend Overlook, where the river curves round by 270 degrees. This is one of the most spectacular and oft-photographed places in Arizona.

Trail to the Bend and Overlook

The overlook at Horseshoe Bend is served by a large parking area on the west side of US 89, five miles south of Glen Canyon Dam; big enough to accommodate the tour buses that often stop here, though increasing popularity often means that all spaces are taken by late morning. The path to the overlook is 1.8 mile roundtrip with 380fft elevation gain though most have little trouble making the hike. The wide path climbs a little to a low, flat-topped hill then descends gently down the far side, across a mix of slickrock and dunes to the unfenced overlook, on the rim of cliffs that are quite sheer for most of the 1,100 foot drop to the river below. Most people stick main rim area to get pictures of the bend symetrically centered which perspective is seen in most photographs though there are multiple great viewpoints all along the rim. Several also take a short walk to the a higher cliff north of the main rim area for elevated views of the bend. There are often several rafts and boats traversing the river that in view from one of the outlooks.
