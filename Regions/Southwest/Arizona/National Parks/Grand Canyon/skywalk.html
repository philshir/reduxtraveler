Grand Canyon Skywalk, Hualapai Indian Reservation

Southwest > Arizona > Grand Canyon NP > Grand Canyon Skywalk

Highlights:

This 10-foot wide, horseshoe-shaped glass bridge extends 70 feet out over the rim of the Grand Canyon. Look down and you can see right through the glass platform 4,000 feet to the floor of the Canyon below.

How to Get There

From South Rim (242mi) Take Center Rd to AZ-64 S. Continue to Tusayan. Get on I-40 W in Williams. Follow I-40 W to Stockton Hill Rd in Kingman. Take exit 51 from I-40 W. Continue on Stockton Hill Rd. Take Diamond Bar Rd to Eagle Point Service Rd.
From KIngman Head south on N 4th St toward E Andy Devine Ave/W Historic Rte 66. Take Stockton Hill Rd to Diamond Bar Rd. Follow Diamond Bar Rd to Eagle Point Service Rd.

The Skywalk

Grand Canyon West has increased in popularity after the opening of the nearby Grand Canyon Skywalk, where visitors can walk along a curved bridge with a glass-bottom walkway projecting 70 feet out over a section of side canyon with near vertical walls, and so look down towards the Colorado River, nearly 4,000 feet below (though the river itself is not directly beneath, but rather about a mile to the west). Also has restaurant that offers American cuisine and offers panoramic views of the Skywalk and Eagle Point. Eagle Point, which was named after a natural rock formation that looks like an eagle. You can watch Native American dances in the amphitheater, visit the Native American Village, and shop at Creations by Native Hands located by the Native American Village.

Cameras and cell phones are not permitted on the Skywalk. the official reason being to protect the glass surface should they be dropped. This rule is enforced by security guards and metal detectors. Pictures may be taken of the skywalk from the rim. Photographers will be available on the Skywalk for guests. Photos can be purchased for $16 per printed photo or $69.90 for a USB drive of all digital photos.
