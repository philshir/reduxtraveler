Camelback Mountain

Southwest > Arizona > Phoenix > Camelback Mountain

Highlights:

As one of the highest peaks in Phoenix, Camelback Mountain in the Scottsdale suburb is among the most scenic hiking spots in the city. Soaring more than 2,700 feet in elevation, Camelback's summit offers spectacular views of Phoenix and Scottsdale and can be reached from two popular trails.

How to Get There

From Phoenix to Echo Canyon Trailhead (14mi) Get on I-10 E. Continue on I-10 E. Take AZ-51 N to E Glendale Ave. Take exit 5 from AZ-51 N. Take E Lincoln Dr to N Echo Canyon Cir.
From Phoenix to Cholla Trailhead (14mi) Take I-10 E. Use the 3rd from the right lane to take exit 147A-147B for AZ-51 N. Continue onto AZ-51 N. Take exit 3 for Indian School Rd. Turn right onto E Indian School Rd. Turn left onto N 32nd St. Turn right onto E Camelback Rd.

The Mountain

Camelback Mountain is a very popular trail located in the center of one of Phoenix's more affluent surburb, Scottsdale. The English name is derived from its shape, which resembles the hump and head of a kneeling camel. The mountain can easily be seen all around the city is very busy due to its location. The most common Flora on the Mountain are the Saguaro Cactus, Ocotillo, Palo Verde, Cholla, Barrel Cactus and the Creosote Bush. There are 2 hiking trails, Echo Canyon and Cholla. Both trails are difficult, while Echo Canyon is steeper and Cholla is longer. The Cholla Trail on the east side of the mountain offers a more gradual incline, at least until you near the summit. Parking at Cholla can be less of a challenge, as you will park on Invergordon Road (aka 64th St) and walk up Cholla Lane. Once parked you will need to walk about 1/4 to 1/2 mile to reach the trail.

The first mile or so is trail hiking. However, once you reach "the saddle" the hike becomes more intense and predominantly scrambling for the final stretch. You can also try one of the several beginner-friendly trails that circle Camelback's base, such as the Bobby's Rock Trail. Hiking Camelback Mountain is best attempted earlier in the morning or later in the afternoon when the desert heat is bearable. But no matter when you decide to climb, make sure you have plenty of water and sunscreen. Locals have warned that the parking lot closure at sunset is strictly enforced and police have issued tickets for late departures.
