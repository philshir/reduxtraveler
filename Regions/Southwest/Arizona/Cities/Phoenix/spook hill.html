How to Get There

Get on I-10 E. Take AZ-202Loop E to E Brown Rd in Mesa. Take exit 26 from AZ-202 S. Drive to E Brown Rd.

The Hill

In the eastern section of the Phoenix suburb of Mesa is a tall, unnamed hill that is easily accessible, provides great views of the surrounding city and mountains, and a good workout hike to the top. Though the hill has no official name, locals often refer to it as Spook Hill after a park a mile Northwest of the location. A search on the AllTrails website shows the trail listed as Brown Road Hill though on their map it is reffered to as Spook HIll and on Google Maps the hill is identified as Brown Mountain though the trail is called Red Mountain Trail. The hill once housed a microwave tower at the but has since been removed and the former 4x4 access road to the tower now serves as the actual trail to the summit. Aside from the naming confusion the trail is easily accessed from Arizona State Route 202 and E Brown Road. There is a small parking lot at base of hill and on busy days you will see several cars parked along the road nearby.

The Trail and Summit

At the trailhead there is a gate that blocks the former 4x4 access road from vehicles though the early part of the trail is somewhat steep and worn. There are private property notices posted, likely from its time serving as a microwave tower, but the trail remains very popular and there are no reports of any issues accessing the trail. After a the trail turns left and becomes less steep and you can already get great views of nearby domineering and impressive Supersition Mountains. After a few more yards of raising, curving paths you will reach the summit and be rewarded with specatular views of the surrounding city framed by several mountains, including the Usery and Goldfield Mountains and on a clear day you can see mountains as far off as Camelback in Scottsdale. The view is even more specatacular at sunset. This is a great hike for those that dont want to make the long trek to larger mountains and longer hikes outside the city suvburbs but still want a good workout and great views of the area.
