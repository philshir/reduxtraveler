Phoenix

Southwest > Arizona > Phoenix

HIGHLIGHTS: The capital and most-populous city in Arizona also the fifth-most populous city in the United States. Phoenix combines vibrant red mountains and cacti-lined boulevards with high-end shopping and numerous local restarunts with varied cuisine from around the world. "The Valley of the Sun," also lures adventurous travelers with its unique desert landscape and numerous hiking trails. Phoenix is said to have the best urban hiking in the nation. It is completely surrounded by mountains and has a small range running right through the city. Phoenix is known by the people of Arizona as “The Valley of the Sun,” or simply, “The Valley,” since it sits in a very large valley with mountain ranges on all sides. These ranges add to the hiking available to Phoenix.

Map of Phoenix

Interactive Phoenix map, showing all parks, preserves, trails and other locations

Black Mouintain

Located in the Phoenix suburb of Carefree, the mountain provides a short but steep hike to the summit containing excellent viewpoint of the surrounding areas.

Buffalo Ridge Park

Small park in Northern Phoenix, provides several short, easy and fun trails to excellent high mountain viewpoits of Phoenix suburban areas.

Camelback Mountain

As one of the highest peaks in Phoenix, Camelback Mountain in the Scottsdale suburb is among the most scenic hiking spots in the city. Soaring more than 2,700 feet in elevation, Camelback's summit offers spectacular views of Phoenix and Scottsdale and can be reached from two popular trails.

First Christian Church

Local church designed by world famous architech Frank Lloyd Wright showcasing eccentric design and towering spire.

Frank Lloyd Wright Spire

An aqua blue, 125-foot steel and glass spire designed by Frank Lloyd Wright is an iconic Scottsdale landmark.

Goldfield Ghost Town

Popular preserved ghost town site in eastern Phoenix at the start of the Apache Trail with a backdrop of the massive Supersition Mountains.

Hayden Butte

A large butte of volcanic origin, located partially on Arizona State University's Tempe campus in Tempe, Arizona. It is often referred to by locals as A Mountain, after the 60-foot-tall gold-painted letter 'A' near the top.

McDowell Mountain Preserve

Located about twenty miles north-east of downtown Phoenix, and seen from most places throughout the city, the area, composed of a playground of white granite domes and spires, Arizona's miniature Joshua Tree is one of the most popular hiking areas in the Phoenix metropolitan area.

Papago Park

Comprised primarily of sandstone, the range is known for its massive buttes that rise and fall throughout the park. The trails that interlace the area are generally easy treks. The smooth footing and low elevation gain make it a popular place for running and mountain biking. Papago Park is home two of the regions most visited attractions, the Phoenix Zoo and the Desert Botanical Garden.

Piestewa Peak

The second highest point in the Phoenix Mountains, after Camelback Mountain, and the third highest in the city. It is located in the Piestewa Peak Recreation Area within the Phoenix Mountain Preserve.

Red Mountain Park

Green space housing facilities for sports from baseball to volleyball, plus picnicking sites.

Riverview Park

Home to the Cubs Spring Training Facility, Sloan Park, and the park itself has been transformed into a premier recreation destination.

San Tan Mountains

Over 10,000 acres of rocky hills and desert flats southeast of Phoenix, protected as a regional park, relatively unspoilt and lightly visited, with plenty of saguaro and other cacti. Crossed by 20 miles of intersecting, well maintained trails, accompanied by a good range of visitor facilities

South Mountain Preserve

Largest city park in the US - a rocky mountain ridge, within Phoenix city limits yet still relatively unspoilt, covered by many cacti and giving excellent views across the urban surroundings. 60 miles of trails, popular for hiking, cycling and horse riding.

Spook Hill

Great views of superstition mountains and East Mesa and can see several other Phoenix mountains from the top.

Superstition Mountains

Rough, rocky mountains east of Phoenix, covered by saguaro and other cacti, and split by many canyons. Excellent hiking opportunities. Contain several lakes and camping areas.

Tonto National Forest Granite Reef and Water Users Recreaction Area

Sitting next to the Lower Salt River, Granite Reef offers the ability to launch non-motorized boats along with shoreline fishing. This riparian area is outstanding for bird watching and fishermen use this area year-round.

Usery Mountains

Small park with good facilities, containing several square miles of Sonoran Desert flats and hills, crossed by many trails, two of which continue into the higher ground of Pass Mountain to the east; most popular is the Wind Cave Trail.

White Tank Regional Park

46 square miles of an isolated, unspoilt mountain range west of Phoenix, home to many wildflowers and other Sonoran Desert plants. The park is crossed by a network of long and short trails, and is also popular for camping, cycling and picnicking Trails.
