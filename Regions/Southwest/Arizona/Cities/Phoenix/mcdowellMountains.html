How to Get There

To Tom's Thumb and East End Summit Trailhead (36 mi) Get on I-10 E. Take AZ-51 N and AZ-101 Loop E to N Hayden Rd in Scottsdale. Take exit 35 from AZ-101 Loop E. Continue on N Hayden Rd to trailhead.
To Thompson Peak trailhead (33 mi) Take AZ-51 N and E Shea Blvd to E Golden Eagle Blvd/Mountain Pkwy in Fountain Hills. Continue on E Golden Eagle Blvd/Mountain Pkwy. Drive to E Desert Tortoise Trail.
To Pinnacle Peak (33 mi) Get on I-10 E. Take AZ-51 N and AZ-101 Loop E to N Hayden Rd in Scottsdale. Take exit 35 from AZ-101 Loop E. Continue on N Hayden Rd to trailhead.

History and Name

The McDowell Mountains are a large mountain range Northwest of Scottsdale that include several varied and challenging hiking trails. The city of Scottsdale has made its share of the McDowells a preserve, and has set up a wide trail network in partnership with the McDowell Sonoran Conservancy. The highest peak in the McDowells is East End, at 4,069 feet. The McDowells also comprise popular landmarks such as Pinnacle Peak and Tom's Thumb. Over the past 20-plus years, the city of Scottsdale has incorporated much of the range into the city boundaries, creating the McDowell Mountains Preserve with the express intent to protect the mountains from development. The range is crossed by an extensive trail network with two several main trailheads that allow for a variety of options, including out-and-back, loop and shuttle hikes. The McDowell Mountains also have some of the best climbing near Phoenix, and arguably the best granite in the entire state with hundreds of routes throughout the range from trad multi-pitch, sport and bouldering, there are climbs for every skill level. The most iconic of any of the rock features is Tom's Thumb, a 200' granite dome perched high on the ridgeline above downtown Scottsdale, and one of the highest points in the range. Although Gardener's Wall does offer longer multi-pitch routes, Tom's Thumb's variable route options, open summit, and 360 views make it the premier piece of rock in the range.
