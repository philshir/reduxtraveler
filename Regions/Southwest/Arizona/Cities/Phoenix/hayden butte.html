Hayden Butte

Southwest > Arizona > Phoenix > Hayden Butte

Highlights:

A large butte of volcanic origin, located partially on Arizona State University's Tempe campus in Tempe, Arizona. It is often referred to by locals as A Mountain, after the 60-foot-tall gold-painted letter 'A' near the top.

From Phoenix (11mi) Get on I-10 E. Continue on I-10 E. Drive from AZ-202Loop E to Tempe. Take exit 6 from AZ-202Loop E. Take W Washington St and N Mill Ave to trailhead.

History and Name

Though Tempe Butte is the official name of the rising butte that can easily bee seen from bust AZ-202 loop, the more common name of Hayden Butte got its name from Charles Trumbull Hayden, who is often credited as being the founder of Tempe. It is also refered to as A Butte due to its proximity to Arizona State University. ASU first became involved with the butte in 1917, when students of the Tempe Normal School (what is now ASU) installed the first letter on Tempe-Hayden Butte: a large “N.” In 1925, the "N" became a "T," as Tempe Normal School changed its name to Tempe State Teachers College. In 1955, when the college was named Arizona State University, a new "A" was built. It remains today. The reinforced steel and poured concrete "A" measures 60 feet from base to top.

The Trail and View

The butte offers wonderful views of Tempe and the surrounding cities, Papago Mountains, Camelback Mountain, Rio Salado or Salt River and much more. The Leonard Monti Trailwinds up the side of Hayden Butte. The highest point of Tempe Butte stands at 1,495 feet in elevation, while its base is at approximately 1,150 feet in elevation. Tempe Butte is most often seen as the backdrop to games held in Sun Devil Stadium, including until recently the Tostitos Fiesta Bowl, as well as Super Bowl XXX.
