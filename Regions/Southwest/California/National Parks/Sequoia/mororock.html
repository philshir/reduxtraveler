Moro Rock

Southwest > California > Sequoia NP > Moro Rock

Highlights:

A rocky outcrop high above the canyon of the Middle Fork of the Kaweah River that has stunning views over many miles of the Sierra Nevada mountains.

General Information

Length
0.4 miles roundtrip
Elevation Gain
187 feet
Pet Policy:
No pets allowed on trails
Seasons:
Spring, summer, fall
Rating (1-5):
★★★★

How to Get There

Half of the trail runs close to a side road; most people prefer to drive, so the walk is pleasingly empty. The path starts at Giant Forest Village and follows the top of a ridge that has great views westwards, over Sequoia Creek and the entrance road. It passes various large trees, and a side-track to Hanging Rock - from this exposed cliff there are more extensive views of the canyon below. After 1.5 miles, path meets road at a parking area close to Moro Rock, from where begins a short but steep ascent, using many steps carved into the surface. The rock is a large exposed granite dome, a feature quite common in the Sierras - most famous is Half Dome in Yosemite Park. At the top, the whole length of Middle Fork canyon can be seen, from low, hazy foothills at the western end to the snow-capped peaks of the Great Western Divide at the head of the valley to the east.
