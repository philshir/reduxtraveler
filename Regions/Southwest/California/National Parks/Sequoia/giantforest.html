Giant Forest/ Parker Group

Southwest > California > Sequoia NP > Giant Forest/ Parker Group

Highlights:

The largest sequoia groves in Sequoia National Park, and most of the visitor facilities, including a good network of trails, to viewpoints, meadows, ponds and peaks, and innumerable ancient trees.

General Information

Pet Policy:
No pets allowed on trails
Seasons:
Spring, summer, fall
Rating (1-5):
★★★★

How to Get There

The Giant Forest forms the heart of Sequoia National Park - here are found the largest trees on Earth, including General Sherman, which holds the world record for the most massive living thing. An area of just 3 square miles contains dozens of magnificent groves of sequoias, most of them easily accessible next to the main road. An extensive network of trails provide access to many peaceful places amongst the trees, and to other scenic features like meadows, streams, fallen logs, granite domes and mountain viewpoints.

Giant Forest Village

The giant sequoia trees are first encountered on the approach from the south at Giant Forest Village - a small cluster of buildings that is dwarfed by the surrounding trees. The sequoia forest extends mainly east of the village, and is accessed by a 3 mile side road plus many interconnecting foot trails. These range from 1 to 5 miles in length, leading to various named individual trees and clusters of particular importance. The area is generally quite sheltered and receives high rainfall, which is one reason why the sequoia attain such size. The paths are generally quite level and well shaded from the sun.

Tunnel Log

The road continues to Crescent Meadow, a large field ringed by sequoias and filled with wildflowers and grass. En route is the Tunnel Log, a fallen tree that has been hollowed for cars to drive through (westbound) - the only such tree in the park. Although Sequoia National Park is open year round, the road to Moro Rock and Crescent Meadow is closed during winter.
