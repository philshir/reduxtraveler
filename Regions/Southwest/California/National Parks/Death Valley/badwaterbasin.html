Badwater Basin

Southwest > California > Death Valley NP > Badwater Basin

Highlights:

Salt deposits and shallow pools at the lowest place in Death Valley, and in the western hemisphere. A roadside overlook provides a viewing point, from where visitors can take short off-trail walks out onto the salt flats. The low, salty pool at Badwater, just beside the main park road 18 miles south of Furnace Creek, is probably the best known and most visited place in Death Valley.

General Information

Pet Policy:
No pets allowed on trails
Seasons:
All year, since little or no hiking is required to view the area
Location:
Parking area on the west side of the highway, 17 miles south of Furnace Creek
Rating (1-5):
★★★★

The Viewpoint

There is not much else at the Badwater viewpoint apart from an orientation table, identifying many of the surrounding mountains. High in the rocky cliffs above the road, another sign reads 'SEA LEVEL', giving a good indication of just how low the land is. Far above this, the overlook at Dante's Peak has imposing views over Badwater and the surrounding desert.

Salt Pools

Several salt trails and shallow seasonal streams lead towards other pools out across the valley. During occasional rainy periods, a large shallow lake forms, several miles across and only a few inches deep, but most of the water soon evaporates or sinks below ground. Badwater never dries out completely, and even manages to support a unique species of fish - the Death Valley pupfish, a small bluish creature which has evolved to survive in the hot saline conditions. South of the salt pools, the seasonal Amargosa River meanders for 30 miles via several routes towards the mouth of the valley, before sinking into the sand.
