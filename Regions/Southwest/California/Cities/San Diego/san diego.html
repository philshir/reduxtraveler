San Diego

Southwest > California > San Diego

HIGHLIGHTS: Consistently sunny weather and 70 miles of magnificent coastline are what draw active types and sun seekers alike to San Diego throughout the year: that and one of the country's favorite zoos. And then there are the beaches: Retreat to Mission Beach to catch a wave, to La Jolla to soak up the sun and to Coronado for a leisurely seaside stroll.

Map of San Diego

Interactive San Diego map, showing all parks, preserves, trails and other locations

Balboa Park

Sprawling outdoor destination is home to the city's renowned zoo, 17 museums, gardens & more.

Cabrillo National Monument

Great views of San Diego Bay, wildflower-covered hillsides, whale watching, an historic lighthouse and tidepools along a rocky coastline.

Coronado Beach

Swimming, sunbathing, surfing & other outdoor recreation draws crowds to this scenic, urban beach and the historic Hotel del Coronado – a 130-year-old National Historic Landmark – is perched just beyond the sand.

La Jolla Cove

Small beach with extraordinary beauty, tucked between sandstone cliffs. One of the most photographed beaches in Southern California. With small surf in the summer months, ideal for swimmers, snorkelers and scuba divers. Water visibility at the Cove can sometimes exceed 30 feet.

Maritime Museum of San Diego

Waterfront venue showcasing many types of restored vintage ships & historical exhibits.

Torrey Pines State Natural Reserve

Dunes, beaches, wildflower-covered hillsides, eroded sandstone cliffs and scattered groves of the rare torrey pine. A popular location, due to the proximity to La Jolla and San Diego.

USS Midway Museum

Life-at-sea exhibits, restored planes & flight simulators aboard this legendary aircraft carrier.
